About Me

I am a Data Scientist with a Ph.D. in Biochemistry, where my research focused on computational physics-based approaches to proteins and drug discovery. In the course of my doctoral work, I developed Python-based workflows to automate complex analyses and worked extensively with large-scale molecular dynamics simulations, often handling datasets of over 100,000 frames. These experiences sharpened my ability to manage high-dimensional data and build efficient pipelines.

Building on this foundation, I am now pursuing a transition into data science, expanding my skills through self-directed projects in machine learning. By combining my scientific rigor with newly acquired ML knowledge, I continue to develop predictive models and analytical tools that connect computational methods with practical insights.

Beyond technical skills, I take pride in being thorough, reliable, and eager to learn. I approach challenges with both persistence and creativity, motivated by the belief that thoughtful data science can drive meaningful scientific and real-world impact.
